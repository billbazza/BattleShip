# 1. GBP Category Audit

**This is the foundation — do it first.**  
Most businesses pick a vague or wrong primary category and wonder why they’re invisible in the map pack.

Your primary category is Google’s #1 signal for relevance (per multiple 2026 local ranking reports). It tells Google what your business *fundamentally is*. Secondary categories (up to 9) expand reach for related searches without diluting your core.

Wrong primary = you don’t even get considered for many searches.  
Competitors almost always have a more specific or better-matched primary.

## The Prompt (copy-paste ready)

"Open Chrome and go to my Google Business Profile at [URL] and these competitors: [URL1], [URL2], [URL3].  
For each listing, extract: primary category and all secondary categories listed.  
Put everything in a spreadsheet. Columns: category name, type (primary/secondary), my listing (yes/no), competitor 1 (yes/no), competitor 2 (yes/no), competitor 3 (yes/no).  
Highlight every category my competitors have that I'm missing (especially if it's their primary).  
Then:  
1. Recommend my ideal primary category — the single most specific one that matches my core revenue service and top search intent in this market.  
2. Recommend up to 9 secondary categories I should add (only relevant ones — no spam).  
3. List any categories that ALL top competitors share — those are table stakes for visibility.  
Base recommendations on current Google guidelines and what drives Local Pack rankings in 2026: specificity over broadness, relevance to searcher intent."

## Why This Matters

Primary category is frequently ranked as the top local ranking factor — it determines whether Google even shows you for core searches like "[service] near me."  
Getting this right can deliver faster, bigger ranking jumps than almost anything else.  
The competitor comparison reveals quick wins: swap to a more specific primary, add missing secondaries that competitors use, and you often see impressions rise within days/weeks.

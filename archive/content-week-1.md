**Facebook version:**

If you're over 45 and thinking about getting fit again — stop thinking about HIIT.

Here's why Zone 2 walking beats it, especially for men with desk jobs and bad knees:

**HIIT (high intensity interval training):**
- Burns calories in the short window
- Spikes cortisol (stress hormone — already too high if you sit at a desk all day)
- Punishes knees, hips, and a back that's been bent over a screen for 20 years
- Most men over 45 can't sustain 3× HIIT sessions a week alongside work and family
- And after 3 weeks of it feeling brutal: they stop

**Zone 2 walking (10,000 steps, brisk pace):**
- Burns fat directly as fuel (your body prefers fat over glycogen at this pace)
- Improves insulin sensitivity — which means less visceral belly fat over time
- Builds aerobic base and mitochondrial health (the stuff that gives you energy)
- Zero injury risk. You can do it with bad knees. In work shoes. On a lunch break.
- It's sustainable forever — because it doesn't destroy you

The science is clear: for men in their 40s and 50s, Zone 2 cardio drives more fat loss long-term than HIIT, with a fraction of the injury and dropout risk.

The reason most men don't do it? It feels "too easy." They think if they're not suffering, it's not working.

That thinking is exactly why they keep stopping.

If you're thinking about a proper reset — start with 10,000 steps a day, brisk pace, for 4 weeks. Track your waist. See what happens.

**Facebook version:**

Most fitness coaches won't touch the alcohol conversation.

Either they demand you quit cold turkey (and you do, for two weeks, then don't), or they pretend it doesn't matter and let it quietly sabotage your results.

I'm going to be straight with you about what alcohol actually does — not to shame you, just so you have the full picture.

**What happens when you drink:**

1. Alcohol is processed by your liver as a priority. While it's doing that, fat burning stops. Completely. Your body won't touch stored fat until the alcohol is cleared.

2. Even 2–3 pints adds 400–600 empty calories with zero protein, zero nutrition, zero satiety. They go straight to visceral belly fat — the deep, dangerous stuff around your organs.

3. Sleep quality tanks. Even if you fall asleep fine, alcohol blocks deep sleep and REM. Low-quality sleep = higher cortisol = more belly fat stored = lower testosterone = lower energy. The next-day crash isn't just a hangover — it's a hormonal reset that takes 48–72 hours.

4. Recovery from exercise is impaired. The muscle protein synthesis that happens after strength work? Alcohol blunts it significantly.

This isn't about sobriety. I'm not asking you to quit.

I'm asking you to understand what you're trading off, and make the choice consciously rather than just drifting.

Men who significantly reduce alcohol as part of a structured reset see the fastest results — not because they're being disciplined, but because they've removed the biggest single barrier to fat loss and energy.

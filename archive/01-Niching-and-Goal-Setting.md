# 1. Niching and Goal Setting – Battleship – Midlife Fitness Reset

---

## The Niche

**Who:** Men aged 45–60 in the UK (primary), English-speaking internationally (secondary).

**The specific avatar:**
- 45–60 years old, male
- Desk job or physically sedentary work (sitting 7+ hours/day)
- Has tried fitness before — diets, gym memberships, programmes — and stopped (2+ failed attempts)
- Blames himself: thinks he lacks willpower, discipline, or "it's just not for me"
- Carries visible belly fat (waist 38"+ / 96cm+); may have had GP comments on blood pressure, cholesterol, or blood sugar
- Drinks regularly — not a self-identified "drinker", just social (weeknights, weekends, always an occasion)
- Family responsibilities: partner, kids (teens or adult), possibly caring for ageing parents — fitness always comes last
- Mild to moderate health anxiety: aware things are sliding, ignores it because the solution feels overwhelming
- Hobbies/identity: outdoors, practical, DIY, camping, cycling — but these have gone quiet in recent years
- Income: £35,000–£80,000/year — can spend £150–250 on a programme if he believes it will work this time
- NOT a gym bro, NOT interested in aesthetics for vanity — wants to feel good, have energy, not die young

**The pain:**
- Wakes up tired, feels older than his age
- Belly fat that wasn't there in his 30s — and clothes that don't fit
- Knees hurt, back aches, energy crashes by 3pm
- Keeps meaning to "get back to it" but never does
- Quietly worried about heart disease, diabetes, early death — but hasn't dealt with it
- Feels like he's letting down his family by running on empty
- Comparison to how he used to feel — that used to be enough to get going, but now even that spark has dimmed

**The dream outcome:**
- Lose the belly, feel lighter and more capable
- Energy back — full days without the crash
- Confidence to get back to hobbies (bike, walks, holidays, swimming)
- Better blood markers at next GP check
- Be there for his kids — active, present, not breathless
- Simple system he can actually follow, that fits his real life, with someone in his corner

---

## Why Battleship Wins This Niche

**The reframe that unlocks trust:**
"It failed before because the system didn't fit your life or your age — not because you're weak."

This is critical. Every other coach sells discipline, motivation, and willpower. Battleship sells *system design*. We diagnose why the previous attempts failed (wrong programme, unsustainable calorie cuts, HIIT with bad knees, no accountability loop) and build one that actually fits: desk job, family time, bad knee, the odd pint.

**Why this audience responds to Will specifically:**
- He IS the avatar. Stubborn ex-heavy-drinker, 40s belly fat, failed multiple times himself, works in technology, has the Apple Watch fitness age data, understands systems, tested everything on himself.
- He's not a gym coach or personal trainer in a vest. He's a real bloke who figured it out and has the receipts.
- His tone is British, warm, non-preachy, realistic. He doesn't demand sobriety. He doesn't push HIIT. He understands bad knees. He gets it.

**Battleship's specific differentiators:**
1. Zone 2 walking as the foundation (not HIIT — protects knees, burns fat, actually sustainable)
2. Progressive strength (not programmes that require 6 days/week in the gym)
3. Non-judgmental alcohol guidance (track it, reduce it, understand it — not "quit or fail")
4. Personalised diagnosis report (not a generic PDF — an actual "here's why it failed AND here's what's different")
5. Ongoing check-ins and adjustments (not just a plan that gathers dust)

## Messaging Framework

**Core headline:** "Battleship – Midlife Fitness Reset: Simple system for men 45–60 who've failed before."

**Supporting messages (rotate in content and ads):**

| Message | Context |
|---------|---------|
| "It failed before because the system didn't fit your life." | Reframe (all channels) |
| "You're not weak. The programme was wrong." | Permission to try again |
| "Zone 2 walking, progressive strength, honest tracking. Nothing extreme." | Method proof |
| "Fitness age 17–18 at 47. No miracle. Just a system." | Social proof (Will's own story) |
| "Still see your mates. Still have a pint sometimes. Just not 15 a week." | Alcohol realism |
| "Belly fat, energy, blood markers. Pick the one you care about most." | Goal anchoring |
| "Be there for your kids. Properly." | Emotional outcome |

**Tone guide:**
- British, warm, direct — no American hype, no "crush it" culture
- Self-deprecating where appropriate (Will's own failures = permission slips)
- Evidence-referenced (Zone 2 science, insulin/visceral fat, Dr Jamnadas) but never lecturing
- Non-shaming — never implies the client is lazy, weak, or lacking discipline
- Practical above all: "here's the one thing to do this week"

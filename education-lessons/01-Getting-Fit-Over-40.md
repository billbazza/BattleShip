
# How to Get Fit and Strong Over 40 Without Obsessing Over Details


- **Key Theme:** Stop overcomplicating fitness — focus on 10% of things that give 90% results: consistency + progression, aligned with busy life. For men 30–50+ who’ve tried dieting/exercise but felt defeated.

**Intro **  
- Common problems: Overwhelmed by info, tracking calories/macros, wrong exercises → quitting before results.  
- Biggest difference between successes/failures: **consistency & progression**, not genetics or magic unlocks.  
- Avoid the "90% effort for 10% gains" traps: cold plunges, red light, supplements stacks, peptides, HRV chasing, exact macros, fasting, special equipment, perfect labs.  
- Mindset shift: Blank slate — focus on nutrition, movement, recovery.

**1. Nutrition **  
- Overcomplicated today due to science/deep dives, but hard to control large studies. Extremes (carnivore/vegan) miss the middle ground.  
- Simple rule: **Lean meats, veggies, fruits, tubers** — closest to nature, no processed foods.  
- Why it works: Low calories per volume → eat more without hunger, even in deficit. Reduces BP, cholesterol, insulin resistance, inflammation; reverses type 2 diabetes possible.  
- Example: Primal 60 challenge (pre-agricultural foods) — participants complain they can't eat enough calories, yet lose weight/fat fast.  
- Takeaway: No calorie/macro obsession — nature's foods auto-regulate.

**2. Movement **  
- Two parts: Daily non-exercise movement + intentional exercise 3x/week.  
- **Daily movement:** 10,000 steps (walk, ruck, etc.) — builds consistency without burnout.  
- **Exercise:** Total body workouts 3x/week (e.g. Mon/Wed/Fri). Rotate focus:  
  - **Day 1: Push** (bench press, overhead press, tricep extension, lateral raises)  
  - **Day 2: Pull** (deadlift variation, rows, pull-ups/lat pulldown, bicep curls)  
  - **Day 3: Legs/Core** (squats/goblet squats, leg press, calf raises, core work like planks)  
- Sets/reps: 3–4 sets per exercise, 8–12 reps (progressive overload).  
- Progression: Add weight/reps when easy — aim for failure on last set.  
- Why total body? Efficient for busy men, full recovery between sessions. No splits needed.  
- Takeaway: Consistency over perfection — start basic, align with life.

**3. Recovery **  
- Often overlooked, but 50% of gains.  
- **Sleep:** 7–9 hours/night — critical for hormone balance, muscle repair, fat loss.  
- **Walking:** Beyond 10k steps, add low-intensity walks for mental clarity & extra calorie burn.  
- **No alcohol:** Huge impact — disrupts sleep, hormones, recovery; cut it for best results.  
- Takeaway: Recovery isn't passive — prioritise sleep & walks like you do workouts.

**Implementation into Busy Life **  
- **Schedule:** 3x30–45 min gym sessions/week (home/gym — no fancy equipment needed).  
- **Progression:** Track lifts — increase load 5–10lbs when reps hit easily.  
- **Mindset:** Don't chase perfection; consistency wins. Align with lifestyle (e.g. walk after dinner).  
- **Resources:** Free minimalist workout guide (link in description). Primal 60 challenge for accountability.  
- Final call: Focus on big levers — nutrition (primal foods), movement (total body + walks), recovery (sleep, no alcohol). No obsession needed.

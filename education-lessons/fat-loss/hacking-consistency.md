# How to ACTUALLY Lose Fat: Hacking Consistency

## The plan is worthless without consistency

You can know everything in this programme and still not lose weight if you apply it three days out of seven. Consistency isn't a personality trait — it's a system. The people who stay lean don't have more willpower. They've arranged their environment so that the default choice is the right choice.

## Default meals

The single most effective consistency hack is having 3–4 meals you can make without thinking. Meals you like, that hit your protein target, that take under 15 minutes. When you're tired on a Tuesday night and don't know what to eat, you default to one of these. You don't negotiate with yourself. You don't order takeaway because you couldn't decide.

Write your default meals down. Make them boring on purpose. Boring is repeatable.

**Example defaults:**
- Breakfast: 3 eggs scrambled with spinach and a slice of toast
- Lunch: Tuna with rice cakes, a handful of cherry tomatoes
- Dinner: Chicken thighs with frozen mixed veg and microwave rice

Not glamorous. Sustainable.

## The two-day rule

Miss one day: fine, life happened. Miss two days in a row: a pattern is forming. The two-day rule says you never let two consecutive days go by without doing the thing. One missed workout is fine. Two missed workouts means you're sliding. One bad eating day is life. Two becomes a week.

This isn't about perfection — it's about preventing the slow drift that ends most attempts.

## What to do when life happens

**Restaurant:** Look for a protein source, ask for extra vegetables instead of chips, skip the bread basket, don't drink your calories. You don't have to be the difficult one at the table — just make the best available choice.

**Travel:** Pack protein. A bag of mixed nuts, a protein bar you actually like, or sliced chicken in a container will prevent the airport food court from destroying a week of progress.

**Celebrations, holidays, big occasions:** Eat what you want. Enjoy it. Then return to your defaults the next morning. One evening doesn't undo three weeks. The problem is never the occasion — it's the three days of "might as well carry on" that follow it.

## The psychology of the hard week

There will be a week where nothing goes right. Energy is low, the scales didn't move, you missed two sessions. This is the moment most people quit — not because the plan failed, but because the gap between expectation and reality feels too wide.

This is also the week that separates the men who change from the men who don't.

The scales not moving doesn't mean nothing is happening. Sleep, stress hormones, water retention, and digestive timing all affect the number. What matters is the 4-week trend, not the single Tuesday morning reading.

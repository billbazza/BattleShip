# How to ACTUALLY Lose Fat: Awareness

## The most common discovery

After one week of logging, most men find one or two things that are quietly derailing them. It's rarely the obvious stuff — it's the things they weren't counting.

Common culprits:
- The olive oil poured freely into a pan (1 tablespoon = 120 calories)
- The oat milk flat white on the commute, twice a day (200+ calories)
- The handful of nuts while cooking (300+ calories if you're not measuring)
- The weekend wine (a bottle of red is 600 calories)
- The "healthy" granola for breakfast (often 500+ calories per bowl)
- Finishing the kids' leftovers

None of these are bad. They're just invisible until you look.

## What to do with what you find

Don't try to fix everything at once. Pick the one or two items with the highest calorie count that you'd least miss. That's your first cut.

A large latte every morning is 250 calories. Switch to black coffee or a small oat milk and you've created a 1,500-calorie weekly deficit without changing a single meal. Over 12 weeks that's roughly 2.5 lbs of fat, from one change.

## The foods that are easy to underestimate

**High-calorie foods that don't feel like much:**
- Nuts and nut butter
- Cheese
- Oils and dressings
- Alcohol
- Granola, cereal
- Smoothies and juices
- Sauces (pesto, mayo, teriyaki)

**Foods that are very hard to overeat:**
- Lean protein (chicken breast, white fish, egg whites, cottage cheese)
- Vegetables (almost any)
- Fruit (in reasonable amounts)
- Potatoes (surprisingly filling per calorie)

The pattern: foods that are easy to overeat are usually high in fat AND carbs simultaneously (crisps, biscuits, pizza, pastries). This combination doesn't exist in nature — it's engineered. Natural foods tend to be one or the other.

## This week

Review your first week of logs. Find the top two calorie sources you'd be happy to reduce. Just two. Make that change this week and log again.

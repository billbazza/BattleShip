# How to ACTUALLY Lose Fat: Getting Started

## The plate method works. This goes further.

The balanced plate gets most people most of the way there. But if you want to know exactly what's happening — and have a number to aim for — this is where we go from instinct to intention.

## Your personal calorie target

Fat loss happens when you eat less than you burn. The maths is simple even if the biology is complicated.

Here's a reliable starting point based on your current weight:

**If you're ready to commit and push:**
`Your weight in lbs × 12 = daily calorie target`

**If you find restriction difficult (you said so in your intake):**
`Your weight in lbs × 15 = daily calorie target`

The ×12 number creates a meaningful deficit — you'll lose weight noticeably. The ×15 number is more sustainable if you've struggled with hunger or restriction before. Neither is wrong. The best target is the one you'll actually hit.

Your number was calculated for you in your diagnosis. Use it as a daily guide, not a hard ceiling — some days you'll be under, some days over. The weekly average is what matters.

## Setting up MyFitnessPal

MyFitnessPal is a free app that makes calorie tracking fast and relatively painless. It has a barcode scanner — point it at anything packaged and the calories appear. For whole foods like chicken or potatoes, it has a database.

**Getting started:**
1. Download MyFitnessPal (free version is sufficient)
2. Create an account — skip the automatic goal setup, we'll set your own
3. Go to Goals → set your daily calorie target to your number above
4. Set protein to at least 30% of your calories (the app does the maths)
5. Log everything you eat for the first two weeks — not to restrict, but to see

## The first two weeks are just awareness

Don't try to change anything in week one. Just log what you normally eat and see what the number actually is. Most people are surprised. Not because they're eating badly — but because they genuinely don't know. A coffee with oat milk and a large lunch can account for more than half a day's calories without it feeling like a lot of food.

You can't close a gap you can't see.

# How to ACTUALLY Lose Fat: Closing the Gap

## You know your number. You know where you're starting. Now close the gap.

By this point you've been logging for a couple of weeks and you know roughly what you're eating. Most people find their actual daily intake is 300–700 calories above their target — sometimes more.

Closing that gap doesn't mean eating less food. It means making the food you eat more filling per calorie.

## The three levers

**Lever 1: More protein.**
Protein is the most satiating macronutrient — it keeps you full longer than carbs or fat per calorie. If you increase protein, you'll naturally eat less of everything else without trying. Aim for protein at breakfast, lunch, and dinner. If you're skipping it at any meal, that's where to start.

Target: roughly 1g of protein per pound of your goal bodyweight. If you want to weigh 180lbs, aim for 180g of protein per day. That sounds like a lot until you realise one chicken breast is 50g, two eggs is 12g, and a Greek yoghurt is 15g.

**Lever 2: Cut liquid calories first.**
Solid food fills you up. Liquid calories do not — your body doesn't register a 300-calorie smoothie the same way it registers a 300-calorie meal. Alcohol, juice, sports drinks, fancy coffees: these are the easiest cuts with the least hunger impact.

**Lever 3: Increase meal volume without increasing calories.**
Add more vegetables. A larger portion of broccoli, an extra handful of salad, another spoonful of peas. These add almost no calories and a lot of physical bulk. Your stomach responds to volume, not just calories.

## What not to do

Don't skip meals. Men over 40 who skip breakfast or lunch to "save calories" almost always overeat at dinner and snack through the evening. Three proper meals, structured around protein, beats skipping every time.

Don't drop below your target calorie number by too much. A 500-calorie deficit is productive. A 1,000-calorie deficit makes you miserable, slows your metabolism, and leads to weekend blowouts that undo the week.

Slow and steady wins this race. 1–2 lbs per week is fast. 0.5 lb per week sustained for 12 weeks is 6 lbs of actual fat, kept off.

## This week

Check your protein. Is it at every meal? If not, fix that before anything else. Protein is the single most effective change most men can make.

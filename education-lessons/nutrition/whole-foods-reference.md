# Whole Foods Reference

## The short version

If it grew, swam, walked, or came from something that did — it's a whole food. If it was made in a factory with a list of ingredients you don't recognise, it's processed. Eat mostly the first category, occasionally the second.

This isn't about being perfect. It's about shifting the ratio.

---

## Protein sources

**Best choices (lean, high protein per calorie):**
- Chicken breast / thigh (thigh is fattier but tastier — fine)
- Turkey mince
- White fish: cod, haddock, tilapia, sea bass
- Oily fish: salmon, mackerel, sardines, trout (also great for Omega-3)
- Tuna (tinned in water or brine, not oil)
- Eggs and egg whites
- Lean beef mince (≤5% fat)
- Sirloin / rump steak
- Prawns and shellfish
- Greek yoghurt (full-fat or 0% — both work)
- Cottage cheese
- Quark

**Plant-based:**
- Tofu and tempeh
- Edamame
- Lentils and chickpeas (also carbs — count as both)
- Low-fat dairy alternatives with added protein

---

## Vegetables (eat freely — don't measure these)

- Broccoli, cauliflower, courgette
- Spinach, kale, rocket, mixed leaves
- Peppers (all colours), cucumber, celery
- Green beans, asparagus, mangetout
- Mushrooms, onions, garlic
- Tomatoes (also fruit, doesn't matter)
- Cabbage, Brussels sprouts
- Frozen mixed vegetables — as useful as fresh

---

## Carbohydrates (portion as a fist-sized amount)

**Slow-digesting (preferred):**
- Oats (porridge — plain or with fruit)
- Brown rice
- Sweet potatoes
- New / jacket potatoes
- Wholegrain bread (1–2 slices)
- Wholegrain pasta
- Quinoa

**Fine in moderation:**
- White rice
- White bread
- Regular pasta
- Corn on the cob

---

## Fats (portion as a thumb)

- Extra virgin olive oil (cooking and dressings)
- Avocado
- Nuts: almonds, walnuts, cashews, Brazil nuts
- Nut butters (no added sugar — check the label)
- Seeds: flax, chia, pumpkin
- Oily fish (counts as both protein and fat)
- Full-fat dairy (cheese, butter — in moderation)

---

## Fruit (1–3 pieces per day)

- Berries (blueberries, strawberries, raspberries) — lowest sugar, highest fibre
- Apples, pears — good fibre, moderate sugar
- Bananas — convenient, higher sugar but fine post-workout
- Oranges, clementines
- Grapes and mango — higher sugar, eat less of these

---

## What to mostly avoid

Not banned. Just not helpful when trying to lose fat:

| Item | Why it's a problem |
|------|-------------------|
| Fizzy drinks (including diet) | Empty calories / drives hunger |
| Fruit juice | Same sugar as Coke, no fibre |
| Alcohol | 7 cal/g, lowers inhibitions around food, disrupts sleep |
| Crisps, biscuits, pastries | Fat + carb combination engineered to override fullness |
| Cereals and granola | Often more sugar than you think |
| Sauces: ketchup, BBQ, mayo | Calories add up fast |
| Flavoured yoghurts | Check the label — often 20g+ sugar |

---

## The practical rule

Before you eat something, ask: does this have a protein source? If no — what can you add to make it a more complete meal?

A plate of pasta with sauce is carbs and fat. Add a chicken breast or some prawns and it becomes a balanced meal. One question, one addition.

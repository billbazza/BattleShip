# The Key to Success

## The honest truth about why diets fail

Most men over 40 have tried to lose weight before. They've cut carbs, done Dry January, skipped lunch, bought a Nutribullet. Some of it worked — for a while.

The reason it stopped working wasn't willpower. It wasn't discipline. It was that every approach they tried was designed to be temporary. A "diet" has an end date built in. The moment the end date arrives, the old habits return, and so does the weight.

The key to success is not finding the perfect diet. **It's making the right behaviours boring enough that you do them without thinking.**

## The only three things that actually matter

After all the research, all the conflicting headlines, all the plans that work for some people and not others — three things explain the vast majority of results:

**1. Enough protein.**
Protein keeps you full, preserves muscle while you lose fat, and requires more energy to digest than carbs or fat. If you do one thing, get your protein in first.

**2. Mostly whole foods.**
Not because processed food is poison — but because whole foods are harder to overeat. A bag of crisps is easy to finish without noticing. A chicken breast and some broccoli takes effort to eat past fullness.

**3. A slight calorie deficit, most of the time.**
Not a punishing one. Not a 1,200-calorie starvation plan. A gap between what you eat and what you burn — small enough to sustain, consistent enough to compound.

That's it. Everything else — meal timing, supplements, "superfoods", macros to the gram — is the 20% that gets 80% of the attention and delivers 20% of the results.

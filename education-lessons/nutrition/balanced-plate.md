# Building a Balanced Plate

## No weighing. No apps. No maths.

The plate method is the simplest way to eat well without tracking anything. It works in restaurants, at home, at someone else's house. You just need to look at your plate.

## The formula

**Half the plate: vegetables.**
Any vegetables. Salad, roasted veg, stir-fried greens, peas, broccoli. The goal is volume and fibre — these slow digestion, keep you full, and cost almost nothing calorically.

**A quarter of the plate: protein.**
A palm-sized portion. Chicken breast, salmon, tuna, eggs (2–3), lean beef mince, Greek yoghurt, cottage cheese, tofu. This is the non-negotiable part of every meal.

**A quarter of the plate: slow carbs.**
Rice, potatoes, sweet potatoes, pasta, oats, bread. Not banned — portion-controlled. A fist-sized portion. These are fuel. If you're exercising more, have a bit more. If you had a sedentary day, have a bit less.

**A thumb of fat.**
Olive oil for cooking, butter in moderation, avocado, a handful of nuts. Fat makes food taste good and keeps hormones functioning — don't eliminate it, just don't add it freely.

## What this looks like on a plate

```
┌──────────────────────────────┐
│                              │
│     VEGETABLES (50%)         │
│                              │
├──────────────┬───────────────┤
│              │               │
│  PROTEIN     │  CARBS        │
│  (25%)       │  (25%)        │
│              │               │
└──────────────┴───────────────┘
+ a thumb of fat
```

## Real examples

**Breakfast:** 3 scrambled eggs (protein) + spinach and mushrooms (veg) + one slice of toast (carb) + a scrape of butter (fat)

**Lunch:** Large salad with grilled chicken (protein + veg) + rice or a bread roll (carb) + olive oil dressing (fat)

**Dinner:** Salmon fillet (protein) + roasted broccoli and courgette (veg) + new potatoes (carb) + olive oil (fat)

## What about snacks?

Try not to need them. If hunger between meals is a problem, your main meals aren't big enough — specifically, not enough protein or vegetables. Fix the meals before adding snacks.

If you genuinely need something: Greek yoghurt, a boiled egg, a handful of nuts, or some fruit with cottage cheese. Not biscuits, crisps, or cereal bars — these are designed to make you want more of them.

## This week

Build every meal using the plate formula. Don't count anything. Just look at your plate and ask: is half of this vegetables? Is there a proper protein source? That's the standard.

# The 80/20 Rule

## Stop optimising the wrong things

You've probably seen the arguments online. Keto vs low-fat. Intermittent fasting vs six small meals. Eat before the gym or after. Cut carbs at night. Don't eat fruit because of the sugar.

Here's what the research actually shows: **none of it matters much if you're not getting the basics right.**

The 80/20 rule applied to nutrition means: 80% of your results come from 20% of the decisions. Get that 20% right consistently, and the rest is noise.

## The 20% that delivers 80%

**Protein at every meal.**
Aim for a palm-sized portion of protein three times a day. Chicken, fish, eggs, Greek yoghurt, lean beef, cottage cheese. Before you put anything else on your plate, protein goes on first.

**Vegetables with lunch and dinner.**
Half your plate. Any vegetables. Frozen is fine — frozen broccoli is nutritionally identical to fresh and takes two minutes in a microwave. This isn't about being healthy. It's about volume: vegetables fill you up cheaply, calorically speaking.

**Drinking water, not calories.**
Fizzy drinks, juices, sports drinks, and alcohol are the easiest calories to cut because you don't even register them as food. Switch to water, black coffee, and tea. One change, meaningful result.

**Eating until 80% full, not 100%.**
It takes 20 minutes for your stomach to signal fullness to your brain. Eat slowly enough to catch that signal before you overshoot it. This one habit alone accounts for hundreds of calories a day for most people.

## The 80% you can mostly ignore (for now)

- Meal timing (breakfast vs no breakfast, eating windows)
- Supplements (creatine, protein powder, vitamins — useful but secondary)
- Specific "superfoods"
- Organic vs non-organic
- Exact macros

These things have a place. Once the fundamentals are automatic, you can layer in the details. But not yet.

## This week

Pick one thing from the 20% list and do it every day this week. Just one. Don't try to do all four at once.

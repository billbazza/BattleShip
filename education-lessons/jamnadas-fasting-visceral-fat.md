# Why Fasting Is the Fastest Way to Burn Dangerous Belly Fat

**Lesson from Dr Pradip Jamnadas (Cardiologist)**

If you’ve got a lot of belly fat (that hard, deep visceral fat around your organs), no diet alone will shift it properly until you lower insulin. High insulin locks fat in storage — especially the dangerous kind that causes inflammation, high blood pressure, fatty liver and heart risk.

**Fasting vs Calorie Counting**  
- Cutting calories slowly often backfires: body slows metabolism, burns muscle too, and you feel miserable.  
- Fasting is different: no food → insulin drops fast → body switches to “take money out of the bank” mode → burns stored fat.  
- After ~12 hours without food, glycogen runs out → visceral fat is first to go (the inflammatory belly fat that shows up as a big gut).

**Why This Matters for Men 45–60**  
- Visceral fat is full of inflammatory molecules (IL-6, TNF-α) — unlike fat on hips/bum.  
- High insulin keeps you stuck — even if you eat less. Fasting unlocks the fat fast.

**Actionable Battleship Steps (Start Simple)**  
1. **Beginner (Weeks 1–2)**: Try 12–14 hour fasts (e.g. finish dinner 8 pm, breakfast 8–10 am). Black coffee/tea/water OK.  
2. **Intermediate (Weeks 3–4)**: Push to 16:8 (eat in 8-hour window, fast 16 hours).  
3. **Advanced (Week 5+)**: Add one 24-hour fast per week (dinner to dinner) — only if feeling good.  
4. **Track it**: Log fast windows in MyFitnessPal notes + waist weekly.  
5. **Safety first**: If on meds (diabetes/BP), talk to GP before longer fasts. Stay hydrated.

**Expected Wins**  
- Waist drops faster (visceral fat first)  
- Energy improves once adapted  
- Inflammation down → better BP, joints, mood

**Battleship Tie-In**  
Alcohol spikes insulin too — treat it like a “torpedo” on fasting days. Limit heavily for best results.

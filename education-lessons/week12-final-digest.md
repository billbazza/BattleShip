# The Battleship Reference Guide — Everything That Matters, On One Page

You've made it to Week 12. That puts you in a small minority of people who actually finish what they started.

This isn't a new lesson. It's a single reference document — every principle from the last 12 weeks, distilled to the essentials. Print it, save it, refer back to it whenever you need a reset.

---

## 1. Sleep — the free performance drug

- Bad sleep raises hunger hormones by 24% and stores fat instead of burning it
- **Dark room, cold room (16–19°C), quiet** — these three things alone transform sleep quality
- No food 3–4 hours before bed. No alcohol within 2 hours. Warm shower 1–2 hours before
- Consistent bedtime matters more than total hours

---

## 2. Fat loss — the actual mechanism

- Fat loss happens when you consistently eat less than you burn. That's it.
- **Your number:** bodyweight in lbs × 12 (to push) or × 15 (sustainable)
- Track with MyFitnessPal — the barcode scanner makes it fast
- What to look for: olive oil, oat milk coffees, nuts while cooking, weekend wine — the invisible calories
- One small swap done consistently beats perfect eating done intermittently

---

## 3. Zone 2 walking — the foundation that doesn't stop

- Walk at a pace where you're slightly breathless but could hold a conversation
- Burns fat directly as fuel for the entire duration — not sugar
- Lowers cortisol, improves sleep, reduces blood pressure, compounds over time
- **30 minutes minimum, every day.** This never becomes optional

---

## 4. The 80/20 of nutrition

The 20% that delivers 80% of results:
1. **Protein at every meal** — palm-sized portion, three times a day
2. **Vegetables at lunch and dinner** — half the plate, any vegetables
3. **Drink water, not calories** — cut juice, squash, fizzy drinks
4. **Eat to 80% full** — it takes 20 minutes for your stomach to signal fullness

Ignore for now: meal timing, supplements, superfoods, organic vs non-organic

---

## 5. Visceral fat and insulin — why fasting works

- Visceral belly fat won't shift properly until insulin drops
- Calorie restriction alone often fails here — fasting is different
- After ~12 hours without food, your body switches to burning stored fat (visceral first)
- **Start simple:** finish dinner at 8pm, eat breakfast at 8–10am — that's enough
- Alcohol spikes insulin. Treat it as the enemy on fasting days

---

## 6. Strength training — what it does that cardio can't

- Cardio burns calories during the session. Strength training burns them for 24–48 hours after
- 5kg of extra muscle = ~300 more calories burned daily, permanently, at rest
- The combination that works: **Walking + Lifting + Slight calorie deficit** — all three, not two
- Without lifting in a deficit, up to 40% of weight lost comes from muscle. With it, that drops dramatically
- Training changes your *shape*, not just your size. This is what "toning" actually means

---

## 7. Getting in the gym — the mindset

- Everyone who looks like they know what they're doing was once where you started
- Nobody in the gym is thinking about you — they're thinking about themselves
- **Go at a quiet time. Have a plan. Walk in with purpose**
- Use dumbbells before machines — they build real stability
- The uncomfortable feeling ends at session three. Push through it

---

## 8. The warm-up — non-negotiable after 40

- Cold muscles are stiff muscles. Stiff muscles get injured
- Connective tissue (tendons and ligaments) warms up slower than muscle — it needs the time
- **Structure:** 3–5 mins light cardio → 3–5 mins joint mobility → warm-up sets before each exercise
- Skipping it is how a good training session becomes three weeks of physiotherapy

---

## 9. The Battleship training method — boring wins

- **The biggest mistake: changing your workout every week.** Don't
- Boredom in the gym means your nervous system has learned the movement — that's when muscle growth begins
- Stay on the same routine for 8–12 weeks minimum, adding weight progressively
- The compound movements that matter most: squat, hip hinge (deadlift), horizontal push, horizontal pull, overhead press
- Three sessions per week. 45 minutes. Full rest days between. That's the entire method

---

## The three numbers to know

| What | Your target |
|---|---|
| **Daily calories** | Bodyweight (lbs) × 12 or × 15 |
| **Daily walk** | 30 minutes minimum |
| **Gym sessions** | 3 per week, Mon / Wed / Fri |

## What to do after Week 12

The principles above don't change. The programme does.

- Keep walking every day — this is permanent, not a phase
- Keep lifting three times a week — increase weight when the current weight feels manageable for all 3 sets
- Track calories loosely — you know roughly what your meals cost now, use that awareness
- **Re-read this guide whenever you feel like you're drifting.** It takes 5 minutes and resets everything

You've built the habits. The hard part is done. The rest is just not stopping.

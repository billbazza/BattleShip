# Zone 2 Walking — The Foundation

## What it is
A walk at a pace where you're slightly breathless but can still hold a conversation. Not a stroll. Not a race. Somewhere in between — the pace where your body burns fat directly as fuel.

## Why it's the most important thing in this plan
At rest and at low intensity, your body burns fat. Crank the intensity too high (running, HIIT, boot camps) and it switches to burning sugar instead — and stops when you stop.

Zone 2 walking keeps you in the fat-burning zone for the entire duration. 30 minutes of Zone 2 walking burns more fat than 30 minutes of jogging for most men over 40. It also:
- Lowers cortisol (the hormone that keeps fat on your belly)
- Improves sleep quality
- Reduces blood pressure
- Doesn't stress your joints, knees, or back
- Compounds — the fitter your aerobic base gets, the more fat you burn at rest

Most men skip walking because it doesn't feel hard enough. That's the point. Hard doesn't work long-term. Consistent does.

## The commitment
30 minutes minimum. Every single day. No exceptions unless injured.

This is non-negotiable — not because it's a rule, but because it's the thing that will change everything else. If you walk every day and do nothing else, you will lose fat, sleep better, and feel more in control within 3 weeks.

## How to do it
1. Get outside. Walking indoors on a treadmill works but outside is better for cortisol.
2. Set a 30-minute timer. Walk at a pace where you're slightly breathless — you could talk, but you wouldn't want to sing.
3. Don't check your phone. This is also stress recovery time.

## How to know you're in Zone 2
- You can speak in full sentences but wouldn't want to have a long chat
- You feel warm but not sweating heavily
- Your heart rate is roughly 110–130 bpm (if you have a tracker)
- It feels almost too easy — that's correct

## If you have bad knees or a bad back
Walking is fine and actually therapeutic for both. Flat ground, supportive shoes. Avoid steep hills in the first few weeks. If your knees hurt during or after, shorten the duration but don't stop — 15 minutes twice a day achieves the same thing.

# Push-Up

## What it is
You lower your body to the floor and push back up using your arms and chest. One of the oldest and most effective upper body exercises — no equipment needed.

## Why it's in your plan
Push-ups train your chest, shoulders, and triceps simultaneously. They also require your core to stay rigid, so your abs work every rep. For men over 40, maintaining upper body pushing strength protects shoulder health and posture — both of which deteriorate from desk work. A man who can do 20 clean push-ups has a measurably lower cardiovascular risk profile than one who can't.

## How to do it
1. Place hands slightly wider than shoulder-width on the floor, fingers pointing forward. Feet together or slightly apart.
2. Keep your body in a straight line from head to heels — don't let your hips sag or pike up. Squeeze your glutes and brace your stomach.
3. Lower your chest to the floor (or as close as you can), elbows at roughly 45° from your body — not flared out wide. Push back up to the start.

## What it should feel like
Effort across your chest and the back of your arms. Your core should feel engaged throughout. If your lower back aches, your hips are sagging — squeeze your glutes harder.

## If you can't do a full push-up yet
Hands on a chair or kitchen counter instead of the floor — the higher your hands, the easier it is. This is not a lesser version. This is the correct starting point. Build reps here until the floor version is manageable.

## Progression
- Week 3: knee push-ups or incline (hands on chair) — 3×8
- Week 5: full push-ups from the floor — 3×8
- Week 8+: slow the lowering phase to 3 seconds — same reps, much harder

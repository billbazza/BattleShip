# Goblet Squat

## What it is
A squat where you hold a single dumbbell vertically in front of your chest, like you're cradling a goblet. The weight in front acts as a counterbalance, making it easier to squat with good posture than a regular squat.

## Why it's in your plan
Squatting is the most natural human movement — we just forget how to do it properly. The goblet squat rebuilds that pattern safely. It works your quads (front of thighs), glutes (backside), and core simultaneously. For men over 40, maintaining leg strength is directly linked to testosterone levels, metabolism, and long-term mobility. Strong legs = faster fat burn at rest.

## How to do it
1. Hold one dumbbell vertically at your chest with both hands — grip it like a large mug
2. Stand with feet slightly wider than shoulder-width, toes turned out slightly (about 11 and 1 o'clock)
3. Push your knees outward and sit down and back until your thighs are parallel to the floor (or as low as comfortable) — then drive back up through your heels

## What it should feel like
Effort in the front of your thighs and your backside. Your chest stays upright. Knees track over your toes — they shouldn't cave inward.

## If it hurts or feels wrong
- Knees hurting: don't go as deep. Stop at the point where it's comfortable — even a shallow squat is useful.
- Heels lifting off the floor: your ankles are tight. Put a small weight plate or book under your heels temporarily.
- Falling backward: use a lighter weight or hold a door frame with one hand while you learn the movement.

## Bad knee modification
Keep the squat above 90° — never let your thighs go below parallel. Slow and controlled is more important than depth.

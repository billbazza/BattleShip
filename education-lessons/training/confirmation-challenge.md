# Week 8: What's Your Challenge?

You're eight weeks in.

You're fitter than you were when you filled in that form. You sleep better. You move better. Things that felt hard in Week 1 don't feel hard any more.

That's not an accident. That's the base we've been building.

---

Here's something I've noticed with every man who's done this properly: at some point — usually around now — the question stops being "can I do this?" and starts being "what do I want to do with it?"

The men who keep going aren't motivated by the scales any more. They're motivated by something they want to prove to themselves. A thing that would have seemed completely out of reach the day they filled in the intake form.

Some of the things Battleship men have done in the 12 months after completing the programme:

- **Park Run** — 5km, just to finish. Then again, faster.
- **Open water swimming** — a lake, a sea swim. Just getting in.
- **10km walk with a weighted vest**
- **30-mile cycling day**
- **10km run**
- **Sprint triathlon** — 750m swim, 20km bike, 5km run
- **Half marathon** — walk/run, doesn't matter
- **London to Brighton bike ride** — 54 miles
- **Olympic triathlon**
- **Multi-day hiking** (West Highland Way, Coast to Coast)

Some of these sound impossible right now. That's the point.

---

I'm not asking you to sign up for anything. I'm asking you one question:

**If you could do something in the next 12 months that would have seemed completely impossible the day you filled in that intake form — what would it be?**

Reply to this email. One line is enough.

Whatever you say, we'll keep it in mind as you move into the back half of the programme.

— Will

# What About Arms? Earn Them First.

By now you've been training for five or six weeks. You've done compound movements — squats, hinges, rows, presses. You're starting to feel it. Your body is changing.

And at some point, probably around now, you've thought: *what about arms?*

It's a fair question. Arms are what people notice first. A t-shirt that fits differently. A bit of shape where there wasn't any.

Here's the honest answer — and it might surprise you.

---

## You're already training your arms

Every pulling movement — rows, lat pulldowns, cable rows — is bicep work. Every pressing movement — push-ups, dumbbell press, overhead press — is tricep work.

If you've been doing the compound lifts properly, your arms have been working hard for six weeks. They're responding. You just can't see it yet because fat loss and muscle gain happen at different rates, and the body reveals muscle from the core outward.

Stick with the basics. The arms will come.

---

## Why isolation work too early is a trap

Bicep curls and tricep extensions aren't bad exercises. They're fine. But they're *supplements*, not foundations.

If you swap compound work for isolation work too early, you're trading the thing that builds the body for the thing that decorates it. Men who do this stall. Their arms look pumped in the gym and then disappear an hour later because there's no real mass underneath.

The compound lifts build the mass. Isolation work shapes what's already there.

---

## When to add arm work

You're ready to add dedicated arm work when:

- You've been training consistently for 8+ weeks
- Your compound lifts are progressing week on week (adding weight or reps)
- You have energy left at the end of sessions

At that point, adding 2–3 sets of bicep curls and 2–3 sets of tricep work at the end of a session is perfectly sensible. Not instead of compounds — after them.

---

## The one thing that predicts arm size more than anything else

**Bodyweight.**

Arms don't grow in isolation (no pun intended). They grow when the whole body is in a slight calorie surplus and recovering well. Men who are still in a significant deficit will build very little arm mass regardless of how many curls they do.

Which means: if arm size is your goal, it lives on the other side of the next phase. Fat loss first. Then build.

You're nearly there.

---

## This week

Nothing changes in your training this week. Keep hitting the compounds. Keep the protein up.

But next time you look in the mirror and think *where are my arms* — remember: they're there. You're just not done uncovering them yet.

The blokes who get the arms they want are the ones who didn't skip the boring stuff to get there.

That's you. Keep going.

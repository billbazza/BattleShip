# Gym Terminology 101

## The language of the gym decoded

Nobody explains this. Everyone assumes you know it. You walk in, hear "what's your working set?" or see "4×8 @ RPE 7" written in a plan and feel like an outsider. You're not — the jargon is just jargon.

---

**Rep (repetition)**
One complete movement. One squat down and back up = one rep.

**Set**
A group of consecutive reps before you rest. "3 sets of 10" means you do 10 reps, rest, do 10 more reps, rest, do 10 more reps. Done.

**Rest period**
The time between sets. For strength work, 60–120 seconds. Don't rush it — your muscles need to partially recover to perform the next set properly.

**Compound exercise**
A movement that works multiple muscle groups and joints at once. Squat, deadlift, row, press. These are the most valuable exercises in any programme. You'll spend most of your time on these.

**Isolation exercise**
A movement that targets one muscle group. Bicep curl, leg extension, calf raise. Useful eventually — not the priority in phase 1.

**Progressive overload**
The principle that drives all progress. Each week, you either do more reps at the same weight, or the same reps at a slightly higher weight. Your muscles adapt to stress — if the stress doesn't increase over time, adaptation stops. This is the only reason to go to the gym consistently.

**RPE (Rate of Perceived Exertion)**
A 1–10 scale for how hard a set felt. RPE 10 = you genuinely couldn't do another rep. RPE 7 = you had 3 reps left in the tank. For most of your training, aim for RPE 7–8. You shouldn't be training to absolute failure in most sets — it's not necessary and increases injury risk.

**Failure**
The point at which you physically cannot complete another rep with good form. You don't need to train to failure to build muscle. Stop 1–2 reps short of it.

**Form / technique**
How you execute the movement. Good form means the right muscles are working and your joints are in safe positions. Bad form means something else is compensating — usually something that will eventually complain about it.

**Superset**
Two exercises performed back to back without rest between them. Used to save time. Common pairing: a push exercise followed by a pull exercise (e.g. press followed by row — they use different muscles so one rests while the other works).

**DOMS (Delayed Onset Muscle Soreness)**
The muscle soreness that appears 24–48 hours after training. Not an injury. Not a sign of a good workout (you can make excellent progress without DOMS). Just a side effect of muscles doing work they're not used to. It reduces significantly after the first few weeks.

**Warm-up set**
A lighter set before your working sets to prepare the joint and movement pattern. If you're squatting 60kg, you might do a set with just the bar (20kg) first. Not counted in your working sets.

**Working set**
The sets that count — done at your training weight.

---

## The only number that matters

Progressive overload. Week 1 you squatted 40kg for 3 sets of 8. Week 2 you do 3 sets of 9. Week 3 you do 3 sets of 10. Week 4 you add 2.5kg and go back to 3 sets of 8. Repeat.

Every other piece of gym knowledge is secondary to this.

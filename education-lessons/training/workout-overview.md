# Workout Overview

## You've been walking. Now we add weights.

By this point you've built a base. Your cardiovascular system is working better, your joints are moving, and you've proven to yourself that you can show up consistently. That matters more than anything in the gym.

Strength training is the second pillar. Walking burns calories while you're doing it. Strength training changes your body's composition — it builds muscle, which burns calories while you're sleeping.

Men over 40 who don't lift lose roughly 1% of their muscle mass per year. That doesn't sound like much until you realise that at 50 you're operating on a significantly weaker chassis than you were at 40 — and by 60, daily tasks start to feel harder than they should. Lifting reverses this. It's the most effective anti-ageing tool available and it requires no prescription.

## What your training week looks like

Three sessions per week. That's it.

Not five. Not every day. Three sessions of roughly 45 minutes, with full rest days between. This isn't laziness — it's physiology. Muscle is built during rest, not during the session. The session is the stimulus. Sleep and food are where the adaptation happens.

**Structure:**
- **Session A** — lower body emphasis (legs, glutes, core)
- **Session B** — upper body push (chest, shoulders, triceps)
- **Session C** — upper body pull (back, biceps)

Or, if you're using your workplace gym three days a week: A on Monday, B on Wednesday, C on Friday. The exact days don't matter — the spacing does.

## What you're actually doing in each session

Every session follows the same structure:
1. Warm-up (5–10 minutes)
2. Main lifts (3–4 exercises, 3 sets each)
3. Optional finisher or core work (5–10 minutes)

The main lifts are compound movements — exercises that work multiple muscle groups at once. More on this in the coming weeks. The key point: you're not doing a different workout every session. You're doing the same workout, week after week, lifting slightly more weight each time. That's how progress works.

## How long before you see results

Honest answer: you'll feel different within two weeks (more energy, better sleep, standing taller). You'll look different to yourself in about four weeks. Other people will notice in about eight weeks.

The scale may not move much — muscle is denser than fat, so your weight can stay the same while your body composition improves significantly. Go by how your clothes fit and how you feel, not just the number on the scale.

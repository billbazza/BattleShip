# Found Time — Exercise That Doesn't Cost You a Minute

## The Core Idea

The biggest objection to exercise is time. But most men over 40 already have committed time they aren't counting as exercise opportunity. The goal isn't to carve new time out of a busy life — it's to recognise what's already there.

## Will's London Bridge Example

Three days a week (Monday, Tuesday, Thursday) I'm mandated to be in the office. My train arrives at Euston. My office is at London Bridge — 5km apart.

I walk both ways. That's 10km. 40 minutes each way.

I didn't "find" time to exercise. That time was already committed — I was going to travel that route regardless. I just changed my mode. Weather doesn't matter; I'm doing it no matter what, so the psychological resistance is gone.

Three days a week. Zero additional time cost. ~80 minutes of Zone 2 cardio built into days I was already leaving the house.

## The Workplace Gym

My office building has a basic gym — running machines, Peloton bikes, dumbbells. I didn't join a gym. I use what's already there, three times a week, on the work clock (lunch or end of day before commuting home). It costs me nothing in personal time and nothing in money.

## Why This Matters for Clients

Men in office jobs often say they "can't find time." But:
- A 30-min walk to/from a station is already a Zone 2 session
- A lunch-break gym is on company time, not family time
- A commute-walk removes the weather/motivation variable — you're committed regardless

The reframe: **you're not adding exercise to your life, you're reclassifying time that already exists.**

## Questions to Surface This in Intake

- Do you commute to work? How do you get there?
- Is any part of your journey walkable?
- Does your workplace have any gym or fitness facilities?
- Do you have a lunch break you could use?

## Use in Coaching

When a client says they have no time, ask about their commute first. A 3-day office mandate with a 1km+ station walk is already a programme. Build on what exists before adding anything new.

# Simple Guide: Using MyFitnessPal to Hit Your Calorie Target

You've got a daily calorie target. MyFitnessPal (MFP) is the fastest way to track it — free, takes under 2 minutes a day once you're set up.

Here's exactly what to do.

---

## Step 1 — Download and set up (5 minutes, once only)

Download the free **MyFitnessPal** app (iOS or Android).

Sign up. When it asks for your goal and activity level, ignore what it suggests — **manually set your daily calorie goal** to your Battleship target instead:

Settings → Goals → Calories → enter your number.

That number is in your plan. Use it. MFP's auto-calculation isn't calibrated for this programme.

---

## Step 2 — Log everything

Tap the **+** button → Food. Three ways to log:

- **Search** — type the food name, pick it, adjust portion
- **Barcode scan** — point at the packaging, done
- **Meal photo** — snap your plate, AI suggests the foods

Log drinks too. Coffee with milk, a glass of wine, a squash — they all count.

**Weigh food where you can**, especially things like rice, pasta, cheese, and nuts. Portion guesses are almost always too low.

---

## Step 3 — Save your regulars

Log your usual breakfast once. Then tap the meal name → **Save as Meal**.

Next time: tap **+** → My Meals → add the whole thing in one tap. Most people eat the same 10 meals. After one week it becomes fast.

---

## Step 4 — End of day check

Scroll to the bottom of the diary. It shows your totals and what's left.

You're looking for one thing: are you at or under your daily target?

That's the whole job.

---

## What to expect

The first few days feel slow. By day 5 it's automatic. Most people are logging in under 2 minutes a day within a week.

You don't need Premium. The free version does everything you need.

## This week

Log every meal today — even if you go over. The goal this week is awareness, not perfection. You can't fix what you can't see.

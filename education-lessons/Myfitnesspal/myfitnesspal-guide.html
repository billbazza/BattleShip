Simple Guide to Using MyFitnessPal
for Meal Tracking & Calorie Counting

Beginner-friendly steps • Updated for 2026 app version

1. Get Started (5 minutes)

  • Download the free MyFitnessPal app (iOS or Android).
  • Sign up and enter your details: age, gender, height, weight, activity level, goal.
  • The app sets your daily calorie target automatically (adjust in Settings → Goals if needed).

Example Diary home screen with calories remaining and progress.

2. The Diary Screen – Your Daily Hub

See calories eaten vs. goal, meals (Breakfast, Lunch, Dinner, Snacks), and remaining calories at the top.

Tip: Tap the big blue + button (bottom center) to log anything!

3. Logging Food – Choose the Fastest Way

Tap + → Food to start. Use these options:

  • Search: Type food name (e.g., "Greek yogurt plain") → pick verified entry → adjust portion → add to meal.
  • Barcode Scan: Scan package UPC for instant info (great for packaged foods).
  • Meal Scan / Photo: Snap a pic of your plate → AI suggests foods & portions.
  • Voice Log: Speak your meal (e.g., "oatmeal with banana and almond butter").
Pro tip: Always double-check portion size (use grams/oz for accuracy) — small differences add up!

4. Build Speed & Save Time

  • Recent/frequent foods appear first — tap to re-log quickly.
  • Save repeated meals: Log once → tap meal name → "Save as Meal" or similar.
  • Next time: Search "My Meals" → add entire breakfast in one tap.

5. End Your Day & Review

Scroll to bottom → check totals → "Complete Diary" or just close the app.

See calories/macros feedback and trends in Progress tab.

Quick checklist: Log everything (drinks, oil, condiments). Weigh when possible. Stay consistent!

Quick Recap & Tips

  • Use verified foods (checkmark icon) for best accuracy.
  • Track drinks & hidden calories (coffee creamer, sauces).
  • Free version is excellent; Premium adds ad-free + advanced features.
  • First week feels slow — it becomes habit fast (often <2 min/day).

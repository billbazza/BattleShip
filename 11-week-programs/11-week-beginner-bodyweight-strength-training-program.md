Here is an **11-week beginner bodyweight strength training program** designed specifically for someone who is currently **unfit**, sedentary, and just starting to add **30 minutes of daily walking**. This plan is gentle, safe, and progressive — starting very easy to build confidence, avoid soreness/injury, and create the habit of moving regularly.

**Key principles for this level:**
- **Frequency:** 3 days per week (e.g., Monday / Wednesday / Friday) — keep the other days for walking + full rest.
- **Session length:** 15–25 minutes total (including warm-up and short cool-down stretch).
- **Focus:** Very basic movements → learn form → slowly add reps/time → introduce slightly harder variations around weeks 6–8.
- **Rest between sets:** 60–90 seconds (or longer if needed — breathe and recover).
- **Form first:** Stop if anything hurts (sharp pain). Mild muscle tiredness is normal.
- **Warm-up (every session):** 3–5 minutes of marching in place + gentle arm circles + 5–10 slow bodyweight squats holding onto a chair/counter if needed.
- **Cool-down:** 2–3 minutes of gentle standing stretches (quads, hamstrings, chest openers).
- **Walking integration:** Keep the 30 min daily walks — they are your main cardio. On strength days you can shorten the walk to 15–20 min if tired.
- **Progress rule:** Only increase reps/time/variation when the current level feels manageable (not easy, but not struggling on every set).

**Core exercises (same each session — full-body focus):**
- Wall or Chair Squats (leg strength)
- Knee or Wall Push-ups (chest/arms/shoulders)
- Doorframe or Table Rows (back — or Superman holds if no sturdy surface)
- Glute Bridge (glutes/lower back)
- Bird Dog (core stability & balance)
- Plank hold (on knees or forearms)

**How to progress variations (make harder when easy):**
- Squat → hold 2–3 seconds at bottom → no chair support → add small jump at top (weeks 9+ only if ready).
- Push-up → wall → knee → full → elevate feet slightly (only if knee version easy).
- Row → more upright angle → pause at top → Superman hold with arms/legs lift.
- Glute Bridge → hold top 3 seconds → single-leg version (one leg straight out).
- Bird Dog → hold each position 3–5 seconds → add small arm/leg movement.
- Plank → knees → forearms → add shoulder taps (very late).

Track every session in a notebook or phone notes (reps completed, how it felt, any soreness). If a day feels too hard, repeat the previous week's numbers — no rush.

**Important reminders for unfit beginners:**
- Soreness is normal the first 2–3 weeks — walk lightly, stay hydrated, sleep well.
- If dizzy, chest pain, or joint pain → stop and consult a doctor.
- Pair with your 30-min walks — nutrition (protein + veggies) helps recovery.
- After 11 weeks you'll likely feel much stronger and more capable — then you can move to a slightly more advanced beginner plan.

Consistency beats perfection. You've got this — one session at a time!

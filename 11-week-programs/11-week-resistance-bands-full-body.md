### 11-Week Resistance Bands Full-Body Program

**Frequency:** 3× per week (e.g. Mon / Wed / Fri)
**Focus:** Full-body strength using resistance bands — all major muscle groups, no dumbbells needed
**Equipment:** Resistance bands (light + medium + heavy if possible; a door anchor helps but not essential)

**Why bands work:** They provide constant tension throughout the movement — the muscle works hardest at the top where you're strongest, which is the opposite of dumbbells. For fat loss and muscle tone, they're genuinely effective and underrated.

**Exercises (same order each session):**
- Banded Squat (legs/glutes)
- Banded Hip Hinge / Pull-Through (posterior chain — glutes, hamstrings)
- Banded Chest Press or Push-Up with band across back (chest/shoulders/triceps)
- Banded Row (back/biceps — anchor at door or wrap around a post)
- Banded Lateral Raise (shoulders)
- Banded Bicep Curl (arms — finisher)
- Plank (core — no band needed)

**How to progress without adding dumbbells:**
- Increase reps until you hit the top of the range
- Move to a heavier band
- Slow the eccentric (lowering phase) — 3–4 seconds down makes any band harder
- Reduce rest between sets from 90s → 60s → 45s across the programme
- At Week 7+ you can stack two bands on one exercise for more resistance

**Progression Blocks:**
- Weeks 1–3: 3 sets × 10–15 reps — learn the movements, moderate band
- Weeks 4–6: 3–4 sets × 10–15 reps — heavier band or slow tempo
- Weeks 7–9: 4 sets × 8–12 reps — heavier band + 3s eccentric focus
- Weeks 10–11: 4 sets × 8–12 reps + intensity on final set (slow eccentric or band stack)

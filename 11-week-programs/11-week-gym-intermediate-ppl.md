### 11-Week Gym Intermediate Program (Push / Pull / Legs)

**Frequency:** 3× per week — one Push day, one Pull day, one Legs day per week
**Focus:** Compound free-weight movements, progressive overload, building real strength and muscle
**Equipment:** Full gym — barbell, dumbbells, cables, machines

**Why PPL works:** Each muscle group gets hit once a week with full focus. Push day is chest, shoulders, triceps. Pull day is back and biceps. Legs day is everything from the waist down. Three focused sessions beats three scattered full-body sessions once you're past the beginner stage — you can load heavier and recover properly between sessions.

**The single most important rule of this programme:** Write down your weights after every set. Every session, try to beat the number from last time — one more rep, or 2.5kg more. That's progressive overload. That's what changes your body. Without the log, you're guessing.

---

**PUSH DAY — Chest / Shoulders / Triceps**
1. Barbell or Dumbbell Bench Press (primary compound)
2. Dumbbell Overhead Press (seated or standing)
3. Incline Dumbbell Press or Cable Chest Fly (Week 5+)
4. Lateral Raise (dumbbells or cables)
5. Tricep Pushdown (cable — rope or bar)
6. Overhead Tricep Extension (cable or dumbbell)

**PULL DAY — Back / Biceps**
1. Barbell Row or Dumbbell Row (primary compound)
2. Lat Pulldown (wide grip) or Pull-Up
3. Seated Cable Row (close grip — Week 3+)
4. Face Pull (cable — rear delts and rotator cuff health)
5. Dumbbell Bicep Curl
6. Hammer Curl (Week 5+)

**LEGS DAY — Quads / Hamstrings / Glutes / Calves**
1. Barbell Back Squat or Goblet Squat (if back squat intimidating — start goblet)
2. Romanian Deadlift (barbell or dumbbells)
3. Leg Press (as secondary quad move)
4. Leg Curl Machine (hamstrings)
5. Walking Lunge (dumbbells — Week 3+)
6. Calf Raise (machine or bodyweight on step)

---

**Progression Blocks:**
- Weeks 1–3: 3 sets × 8–10 reps — find working weights, nail form on barbell movements
- Weeks 4–6: 3–4 sets × 8–10 reps — add weight where form is consistent, introduce accessory moves
- Weeks 7–9: 4 sets × 6–8 reps — heavier, lower reps, strength emphasis
- Weeks 10–11: 4 sets × 6–8 reps + last set to near failure — test new maxes

**Starting weights for barbell movements:**
- Bench press: bar only (20kg) first session — feel the movement. Add 5kg each side only when you own 3 × 8 with good form.
- Barbell row: same as bench — bar only first session.
- Squat: bar only or goblet squat with 10–12kg. Do not ego-lift. Form on squats takes time.
- RDL: start light (bar or light dumbbells). The hamstring stretch tells you if you're doing it right.

**Injury notes (apply every week, not just at the start):**
- Lower back on RDL: hinge at the hip, soft knee bend, bar/dumbbells stay close to the legs. If your back rounds, the weight is too heavy.
- Shoulder on bench: don't flare elbows 90°. Tuck slightly — about 70°. Protects the rotator cuff.
- Knee on squat: push knees out in the direction of your toes. Don't let them cave inward.
- If something hurts sharply — stop. Dull muscle burn is normal. Sharp joint pain is not.

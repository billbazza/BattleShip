Here is an **11-week bodyweight HIIT (High-Intensity Interval Training) program** tailored for someone with **low to moderate fitness** who already does some HIIT or has basic cardio/strength experience (e.g., can handle 20–30 minutes of moderate effort without excessive fatigue). This is **no-equipment**, home-based, full-body focused — combining strength, power, and cardio for fat burn, endurance, and muscle tone.

**Key guidelines:**
- **Frequency:** 3 days per week (e.g., Mon/Wed/Fri or Tue/Thu/Sat) — non-consecutive for recovery. On off days, do light walking, mobility, or rest.
- **Session length:** 20–35 minutes (including warm-up/cool-down).
- **Format:** Circuit style — perform exercises back-to-back with minimal rest, then rest after the full circuit.
- **Warm-up (every session):** 4–6 minutes — marching/jog in place, arm circles, bodyweight squats, dynamic lunges, high knees (slow).
- **Cool-down:** 3–5 minutes — walking + static stretches (quads, hamstrings, chest, shoulders).
- **Intensity:** Work intervals at 80–90% effort (hard but sustainable form). Rest/recovery = active (march/walk) or full rest.
- **Progression:** Increase work time, reduce rest, add rounds, or advance variations (e.g., add jump, slow tempo, or explosive version).
- **Modifications:** If too hard, shorten work or extend rest. If too easy, increase pace/explosiveness.
- **Listen to body:** Soreness normal early on; sharp pain = stop. Hydrate, eat protein-rich, sleep well.

**Core circuit exercises (rotate variations as noted):**
1. Squat variation (e.g., bodyweight squat → squat jump)
2. Push-up variation (knee → standard → explosive)
3. Lunge variation (alternating → jumping lunges)
4. Plank variation (standard → mountain climbers → plank jacks)
5. Burpee variation (step back → full → no push-up if needed)
6. High knees or mountain climbers (cardio burst)
7. Core finisher (e.g., bicycle crunches or leg raises)

**Example Weekly Circuit (adapt variations per week):**
- Squat / Squat Jump
- Push-up (knee/standard/explosive)
- Alternating Lunge / Jumping Lunge
- Plank Hold / Mountain Climbers / Plank Jacks
- Burpee (step/full/no push-up)
- High Knees (in place)
- Bicycle Crunches (core finisher — 45–60s continuous)

**Progress tips:**
- Track rounds completed + how many "full effort" reps per interval.
- If a week feels too tough, repeat the prior week's structure.
- Combine with your existing moderate activity (e.g., walking, light cardio) on off days — don't add more HIIT unless recovering perfectly.
- After week 11: Maintain 3–4×/week or progress to weighted HIIT/dumbbells if desired.

This plan builds metabolic conditioning, burns calories efficiently, and improves work capacity progressively without overwhelming recovery at moderate fitness. Stay consistent, fuel properly, and enjoy the energy boost! If joint issues arise, swap high-impact moves (e.g., jumps → fast squats). You've got this — push smart!

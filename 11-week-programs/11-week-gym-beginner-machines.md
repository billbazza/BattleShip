### 11-Week Gym Beginner Program (Machines)

**Frequency:** 3× per week — full body each session
**Focus:** Building the gym habit, learning movement patterns on machines, progressive overload from session one
**Equipment:** Gym membership — machines only for Weeks 1–4, free weights introduced gradually from Week 5

**The most important thing about starting the gym:** You do not need to know what you're doing. Everyone in there was a beginner once. Go in, find the machines listed below, and do the work. Nobody is watching you. The people who look confident are thinking about their own session, not yours.

**First session checklist:**
1. Find the leg press — it's usually the biggest machine in the room
2. Find the chest press machine (seated, handles in front)
3. Find the lat pulldown (cable with a bar you pull down)
4. Find the seated cable row (low cable, two handles)
5. Find the shoulder press machine (seated overhead press)
6. That's your whole programme. Five machines.

**Exercises (same order each session — all machines Weeks 1–4):**
- Leg Press (quads, glutes, hamstrings)
- Chest Press Machine (chest, shoulders, triceps)
- Lat Pulldown (upper back, biceps)
- Seated Cable Row (mid-back, biceps)
- Shoulder Press Machine (shoulders, triceps)
- Leg Curl Machine (hamstrings — add from Week 3)
- Plank or Ab Crunch Machine (core finisher)

**Progression Blocks:**
- Weeks 1–4: Machines only, 3 sets × 10–12 reps — find your baseline weight, don't go to failure yet
- Weeks 5–7: 3–4 sets — add weight where you're consistently hitting the top of the range, introduce dumbbell RDL
- Weeks 8–9: 4 sets — heavier, introduce barbell squat or goblet squat with a weight plate if comfortable
- Weeks 10–11: 4 sets + last set to near failure — track your personal bests on each machine

**How to pick your starting weight:** Set the machine to a weight where the last 2–3 reps of a 12-rep set feel like genuine effort. Not agony — effort. If you finish 12 reps and could easily do 6 more, go heavier. If you can't finish the set with good form, go lighter. Write the weight down after every session.

**Progression to Gym Intermediate:** Once you've completed Week 8 and you're comfortable navigating the gym floor, you're ready to graduate to the PPL (Push/Pull/Legs) programme. This is a bigger unlock — three focused days instead of full-body, free weights as the primary tool, and the ability to load heavier than machines allow. Your coach will flag when you're ready.

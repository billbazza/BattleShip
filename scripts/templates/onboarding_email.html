The Science

Why Walking Is the Fat-Loss Monster Nobody Talks About

At rest and at low intensity, your body burns fat as its primary fuel. The moment you push too hard — running, HIIT, boot camp — it switches to burning sugar instead. Sugar burns fast, then you stop, and the fat stays exactly where it is.

Zone 2 walking keeps you in the fat-burning zone for the entire duration. 30 minutes of brisk walking burns more fat than 30 minutes of jogging for most men over 40. Not because it's harder — because it's the right intensity.

Walking also:

✓ Lowers cortisol — the stress hormone that tells your body to store fat around your belly
✓ Improves sleep quality — especially relevant if you're getting 5–6 hours
✓ Reduces blood pressure — every 30-minute walk has a measurable effect on BP
✓ Doesn't stress your joints, knees, or back — it's therapeutic for all three
✓ Compounds — the more you do it, the more fat you burn at rest, even when sitting

Most men skip walking because it doesn't feel hard enough. That's the point. Hard doesn't work long-term. Consistent does.

How to do it

Zone 2 Walking — The Right Pace

Not a stroll. Not a race. Somewhere in between — the pace where your body burns fat directly as fuel.

1 Get outside. Fresh air beats treadmill. Your cortisol drops faster outdoors. Flat ground, supportive shoes.
2 Find your Zone 2 pace. You should be slightly breathless — you could hold a conversation but you wouldn't want to. Warm, not drenched. Heart rate roughly 110–130 bpm if you have a tracker.
3 30 minutes. Timer on, phone away. This is also your recovery time. The walk and the mental switch-off both matter.

If you have bad knees or a bad back — walking is fine for both. Flat ground, short distance if needed. Two 15-minute walks = the same benefit as one 30-minute walk.

The Reset

A 12-week system for men over 40 who are done making excuses and ready to actually change.

Before we start

This isn't a fitness programme. It's a reset.

Most men over 40 already know what they should be doing. The problem isn't information — it's that nothing has ever been built around the specific version of their life.

This guide covers the four things that actually move the needle: how you eat, how you move, how you sleep, and what alcohol is quietly doing to your body. No fads. No five-AM routines. No gym required.

At the end, you'll find a short questionnaire. The more honest you are with it, the more specific your plan will be. That's the whole point — a plan built around you, not a template.

The structure

Twelve weeks to see real change. Then a lifetime of keeping it.

The Reset works in three phases. Each builds on the last. By Week 12 you won't be finishing a programme — you'll have built the habits that make the rest of your life different.

Weeks 1–4

Foundation

Establish baselines. Build the habits that support everything else — sleep, walking, eating patterns. No dramatic changes yet, just consistency.

Weeks 5–8

Momentum

You'll feel it by now. Strength training introduced or progressed. Nutrition dialled in. Energy starts shifting. This is where visible change begins.

Weeks 9–12

Mastery

The habits are yours now. We push harder, measure what changed, and build the accountability structure that keeps it going beyond Week 12.

After Week 12, clients move into an ongoing accountability phase — lighter check-ins, continued education, and the option to go deeper into strength or nutrition work. The programme ends. The reset doesn't.

The four pillars

What actually makes the difference.

01

Diet

Understand it first,
control it second

You don't need to count calories forever. But the most important thing you can do at the start is understand where the problems actually are. Most men are shocked when they see it clearly for the first time — not because they're eating badly, but because they don't know which meals are doing the damage.

The Reset uses a short tracking phase — not as a permanent habit, but as a diagnostic. Once you can see the pattern, you can fix the pattern. Then you move on.

On intermittent fasting: For belly fat specifically, a simple eating window works well for most men over 40 — not because it's magic, but because it reduces unconscious snacking and late-night eating without requiring willpower at every meal. We'll build this in gradually, not overnight.

The goal isn't restriction. It's eating enough of the right things often enough that your body stops storing everything as fat. Protein is the biggest lever most men aren't pulling hard enough.

02

Movement

Walking is not
a consolation prize

Walking is the most underrated fat loss tool available. It's low-impact, sustainable, doesn't require recovery, and compounds over time in a way that gym sessions alone don't. A man who walks 8,000 steps a day consistently will outperform a man who does three intense gym sessions and sits the rest of the time.

Your starting point doesn't matter. We track your current daily average and build from there — using your phone or a wearable. No specific target until we know your baseline.

Strength training comes second. Once movement is consistent, we add 2–3 structured sessions per week. These can be at home with minimal equipment, in a gym, or bodyweight only. The programme is built around what you actually have access to — not what a gym membership assumes you want.

03

Sleep

The invisible lever
most men ignore

Poor sleep drives fat storage directly. When you're under-slept, cortisol rises, hunger hormones go up (ghrelin), and the hormone that tells you you're full (leptin) drops. You're fighting your own biology every time you try to eat well on six hours of sleep.

You don't need to become a sleep monk. But the basics matter: a consistent bedtime, a room that's actually dark and cool, and cutting the things that actively destroy sleep quality — late eating, screens, and alcohol (more on that below).

Men who fix their sleep first almost always find the other pillars easier. It's not a coincidence.

04

Alcohol

The honest
conversation

This isn't about moralising. Most men over 40 who are struggling with body composition are drinking more than they think, and alcohol is doing more damage than they realise.

Here's what it actually does: alcohol is metabolised before everything else, which means fat burning stops completely while your body processes it. It disrupts deep sleep even in small amounts. It's calorie-dense in a way that doesn't register as food. And it drives hunger the next day.

You don't have to stop drinking. But you do need to understand the trade-off clearly. The Reset will be honest with you about what your current intake is costing you — and give you options, not ultimatums. Some men cut back significantly once they see the numbers. Others don't, and we build a plan that accounts for that.

The questionnaire asks about alcohol directly. It's confidential. The more honest you are, the more realistic your plan will be.
